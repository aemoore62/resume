About Me

I collaborate across the sciences to advance how scientists share complex information. As digital technologies advance—ranging from artificial intelligence to quantum computing—scientists gain new avenues towards insight and innovation. Yet scientists remain challenged to rapidly share science, including the data, metadata, and interpretations that break grounds, transform life, and propel science. I collaboratively reimagine how scientists engage ubiquitous technologies to share complex information about how investigations are conducted.

I hold a PhD in Bioinformatics from University of Georgia (UGA) and a BA in Bioinformatics from Hampshire College. I’m currently transitioning into a brief term as a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Edison Lab at UGA. As a Postdoc, I’ll focus on scientific publications for the suite of technologies that I’m actively developing (see below).

Expertise

Let's advance how scientists share information.

Ideate

I most enjoy originating ideas, both independently and collaboratively. I am keen to identify existing resources and repurpose them in novel ways. I feel brightest when I collaboratively synthesize concepts, technologies, inquiries, and insights that demonstrate the potential of narrowly explored spaces.

Develop

I like to repeatedly grapple with new and contrasting requirements until I've generated and prototyped ideas that highly satisfy users. To do this, I not only take a user-centric approach toward developing digital resources but also take opportunities to literally put myself in the users' workflow. I appreciate collaborating with others to see an app through to production.

Communicate

I love to write and generate engaging visualizations. I enjoy targeting a diversity of audiences, including experts across scientific domains, business stakeholders, and the general public. I enjoy crafting clear, concise narratives that balance scientific insight with context and impact that engage diverse audiences.
